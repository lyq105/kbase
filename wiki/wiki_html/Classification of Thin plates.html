Classifications of thin plates

Defination 1 : Thin plates are initially flat structural memebers bounded by two parallel planes, called faces, and a cylindrical surface, called an eage of boundary.

Defination 2: The distance between the plane faces is called the thickness$(h)$ of the plate.

Plates may be classified into three groups according to the ratio $a/h$, where $a$ is a typical dimension of plates in a plane and $h$ is a plate thickness. These groups are

  1. Thick plates: the ratios $a/h \le 8 \ldots 10$. The analysis of such bodies using the general equations of three-dimensional elasticity.
  2. Membranes: the ratios $a/h \ge 80 \ldots 100$.
  3. Thin plates: the ratio $8 \ldots 10 \le a/h \ge 80 \ldots 100$.

Thin plate may also be subdivided into two different classes by the ratio $w/h$ which is the ratio of the maximum deflection of the plate to its thickness.

The first class is Stiff Plates : A plate can be classified as a stiff palte if $w/h \le 0.2$.

The second class is Flexible plates: if the plate deflections are beyond a certain level, $w/h \ge 0.3$, then the lateral deflections will be accompanied by stretching of the middle seuface.
